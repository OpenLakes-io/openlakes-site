Delivery tiers

Choose the lakehouse package that matches your ambition

Every tier includes architecture, deployment, documentation, and hand-off with your data teams.

OpenLakes Core

Minimum headache lakehouse

Ideal when you just need an on-prem OSS lakehouse you can query quickly. We focus on a single UX surface powered by OpenMetadata so your analysts see lineage, quality, and discovery in one place.

  • Storage: MinIO with Iceberg tables
  • Metadata: OpenMetadata (catalog, lineage, quality)
  • Compute: Trino for ad-hoc/BI, Spark optional
  • Ingestion: Airbyte with optional Debezium
  • Orchestration: Airflow
  • Governance: OpenMetadata RBAC + object-store policies
  • BI: Apache Superset
  • Infra: Kubernetes, Prometheus/Grafana, Vault

Best for greenfield analytics teams that need fast time-to-value.

OpenLakes Enterprise

Full OSS lakehouse control

Built for orgs juggling multiple sources, Data Vault modeling, and governance requirements. Adds streaming ingestion, branching catalogs, and policy enforcement across compute engines.

  • Storage: MinIO + Iceberg
  • Metadata: OpenMetadata plus optional Nessie
  • Compute: Spark ETL, Trino federated/BI
  • Ingestion: Kafka, Debezium, Airbyte
  • Orchestration: Airflow
  • Governance: Apache Ranger policies surfaced in OpenMetadata
  • Quality: Great Expectations + OpenMetadata profiling
  • BI & Infra: Superset, k8s, Prom/Grafana, Vault

For regulated teams that need lineage, controls, and Data Vault done right.

OpenLakes Realtime / Serve

High-concurrency, low-latency lake

Everything from Enterprise plus a StarRocks serve tier for sub-second dashboards, mutable fact tables, and operational analytics. Perfect when Kafka + Debezium already power change data capture.

  • Serve: StarRocks for OLAP + lake queries
  • Pattern: Land ➝ Iceberg ➝ materialize hot marts ➝ serve
  • Workflow: Airflow pipelines feed StarRocks rollups
  • Use cases: Realtime KPIs, product analytics, ML feature stores

Only added when your workloads truly need a dedicated serve tier.
